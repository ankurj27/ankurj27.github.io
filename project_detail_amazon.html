Amazon Prime Video Dashboard

Analyze Amazon Prime Video Content available on the platform from 1920 to 2021.

❮ ❯

Overview

This dashboard reveals a massive and diverse content library, driven primarily by drama, movies, and content targeting mature audiences.

Scale and Scope of the Library

  • Creative Breadth:This content was created by a huge network, involving 5,771 unique Directors across 519 total genres, highlighting a commitment to content diversity and global acquisition.

Content Format Domination

  • Movie Focus: The content library is overwhelmingly dominated by Movies, which account for $7.81K$ of the titles, representing $80.82\%$ of the total. TV Shows make up the remaining $1.85K$ ($19.18\%$).

  • Insight: Amazon Prime's content strategy heavily favors acquiring and producing feature films over episodic television series.

Genre Hierarchy

  • Top Genres:

    • Drama: 966 titles

    Comedy: 536 titles (Note: Comedy and Comed genres appear fragmented/duplicated)

    Insight: Drama and Comedy are the backbone of the Prime Video library, aligning with universally popular and easily consumable content categories.

Release Trend & Content Age:

  • Recent Surge: The line graph for "Total shows by release year" shows a dramatic, near-vertical spike in content acquisition and production in recent years (post-2000, particularly leading up to 2021).

  • Insight: While the dataset spans 1920 to 2021, the vast majority of the 9,655 titles are modern releases, suggesting an aggressive recent strategy to rapidly grow the catalog.

Audience & Rating Strategy

  • Mature Audience Focus: The $13+$ and $16+$ ratings are the most numerous categories, with $2.12K$ and $1.55K$ titles, respectively. The $ALL$ rating comes in third with $1.27K$ titles.

  • Insight: Prime Video is primarily catering to teen and adult demographics, with content aimed at family (ALL) being the third priority. The $R$ (Restricted) category also has a significant presence ($1.01K$).
